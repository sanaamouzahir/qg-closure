SIGMA RECALIBRATION: BEFORE / AFTER
====================================
THE QUESTION THIS FOLDER ANSWERS: how much better does the uncertainty get
if we simply multiply every predicted sigma by one number?

EVERY PNG HAS TWO PANELS: the reliability diagram (empirical vs nominal
coverage, diagonal = honest) BEFORE (left, scale s = 1) and AFTER (right,
sigma replaced by s * sigma).
HOW s IS FOUND: on the FIRST half of the validation window, s^2 = the mean
of the squared standardized errors z = (y - mu)/sigma. A perfectly
calibrated model gives s = 1. s < 1 means the model was over-stating its
uncertainty. The plot is drawn on the SECOND half (honest out-of-sample
test). The fitted s and before/after numbers are in
yamls/sigma_recalibration_before_after/.
INPUTS: best checkpoint + validation frames. The checkpoint is never
modified -- s lives in a side file that consumers apply at read time.

FILES:
  cylinder_steady_production_...png   s = 0.521 (sigma was ~2x too big);
                                      NLL improves 5.16 -> 4.91
  cape_baseline_...png                s = 0.508 (same ~2x over-caution)
  hyperparameter_grid_winner_60epoch_...png  the winner before extension

WHY IT MATTERS: one number fixes the overall size of sigma but coverage
then sticks at ~0.95 -- proof the remaining problem is per-pixel SHAPE
(sigma too flat across the domain), which motivates the gradient-based
noise conditioning now training in the ensemble models.
