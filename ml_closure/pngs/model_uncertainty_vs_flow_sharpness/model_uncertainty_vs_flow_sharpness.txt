MODEL UNCERTAINTY VS FLOW SHARPNESS
===================================
THE QUESTION THIS FOLDER ANSWERS: the real errors are largest where the
flow is sharpest (strong vorticity gradients) -- does the model's predicted
uncertainty know that?

EVERY PNG SHOWS (one log-log subplot per simulation member): pixels are
grouped into ten bins by their local vorticity-gradient magnitude
|grad omega| (x axis). Two lines per member:
  - median PREDICTED sigma per bin        (what the model claims)
  - median ACTUAL |error| per bin         (what really happens)
The subplot title gives the two Spearman rank correlations: rho(sigma,
gradient) and rho(|error|, gradient).
INPUTS: best checkpoint + validation frames; the gradient is computed from
the filtered vorticity input field.
COMPARES: the SHAPE of sigma against the SHAPE of the true error, as a
function of flow sharpness.
PURPOSE AND JUSTIFICATION: this is the picture of the known per-pixel
shape miscalibration -- |error| climbs steeply with the gradient while
sigma stays comparatively flat. It is the evidence behind the structural
noise prior (T6 ladder arm F: sigma^2 = a + b|grad omega|^2) and behind
the gradient feature switched on in the conditioned ensembles now training.
