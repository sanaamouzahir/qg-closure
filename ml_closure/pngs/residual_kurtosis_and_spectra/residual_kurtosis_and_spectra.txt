RESIDUAL SPECTRA (AND THE HEAVY-TAIL STORY)
===========================================
THE QUESTION THIS FOLDER ANSWERS: at which SPATIAL SCALES does the model's
error live -- large structures or fine grain?

EVERY PNG HAS TWO PANELS, one line per simulation member:
  LEFT:  the radial power spectrum of the residual (truth - prediction):
         energy of the error vs spatial wavenumber k (small k = large
         structures, large k = fine detail)
  RIGHT: the ratio (residual power)/(truth power) vs k, with a horizontal
         line at 1. Below 1 = the model captures that scale; near 1 = at
         that scale the prediction adds nothing.
INPUTS: best checkpoint + validation frames (masked pixels zero-filled,
double-precision FFT).
COMPARES: error spectrum vs truth spectrum, scale by scale, member by
member.
PURPOSE: tells us WHAT KIND of miss the remaining error is -- a ratio
rising to 1 at high k with low values at small k means the model gets the
big eddies and misses the fine grain (the expected, benign pattern for a
smoothing closure). A bump at low k would be far more worrying.

ABOUT KURTOSIS: the residual kurtosis quoted alongside these plots
(cylinder ~416, cape ~2064 -- Gaussian would be 3) measures how extreme
the rare large errors are; cape's errors are ~5x heavier-tailed. The
number itself is in yamls/deep_diagnostics_summaries/ and in the
per-member tables in csvs_and_npz/.
