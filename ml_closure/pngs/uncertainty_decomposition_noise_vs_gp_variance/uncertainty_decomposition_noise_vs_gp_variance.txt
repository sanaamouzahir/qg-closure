WHAT THIS DIAGNOSTIC IS (2026-07-13 field-plot triage, item P4)

The model's predictive variance is the sum of two parts:
    total = posterior GP variance (input-dependent, epistemic)
          + likelihood noise sigma_n^2 (ONE global constant)
If the constant dominates, the predicted uncertainty is flat — it cannot mark
where the model is actually wrong. This decomposition measures both parts in
PHYSICAL target units (GP-space values times y_sd^2) per ensemble member,
over up to 24 evenly-spaced validation frames each, for the two frozen
checkpoints.

RESULT
  Cape model, all 5 members IN distribution: sigma_n^2 dominates the
  posterior GP variance by 493x to 1133x. Full collapse to homoscedastic —
  the predicted sigma is one number. This is the "before" evidence for the
  heteroscedastic upgrade.
  Cylinder model: on its own training member the same collapse (577x). On
  the 4 modulated members it never saw, the posterior GP variance inflates
  to 1x-8x the noise (sine 0.2x ratio, ramp 0.1x, ou 1.0x, telS-A 0.1x in
  noise/posterior terms) — the GP does react epistemically to
  out-of-distribution inputs, but within distribution sigma is flat.
  Numbers: sigma_n^2 = 3.64e3 (cylinder model), 3.28e4 (cape model),
  physical units.
