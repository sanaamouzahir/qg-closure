CALIBRATION: RELIABILITY AND SPREAD-SKILL
=========================================
THE QUESTION THIS FOLDER ANSWERS: when the model says "I am this sure",
is it telling the truth?

TWO KINDS OF PNG HERE:

1) *_calibration_and_spread_skill.png  -- two panels:
   LEFT (reliability diagram): nominal coverage on x (what a perfect
   Gaussian model would give at 1, 2, 3 sigma: 68.3%, 95.5%, 99.7%),
   empirical coverage on y (the fraction of pixels whose true value really
   fell inside mu +/- k*sigma). The diagonal = perfectly honest. Above the
   diagonal = OVER-cautious (sigma too big); below = overconfident.
   Dashed lines: the same curve computed separately per inlet-speed bin.
   RIGHT (spread-skill): pixels are binned by their predicted sigma (x);
   y = the ACTUAL root-mean-square error inside that bin. On the diagonal,
   sigma is a faithful error forecast pixel by pixel.
   INPUTS: best checkpoint + all validation frames.
   FILES: cylinder_steady_production, cape_baseline,
   hyperparameter_grid_winner_60epoch, cylinder_smoke_test_run0401/0455.

2) *_reliability_per_member.png -- the reliability diagram alone, one
   subplot per simulation member (inlet type), at eight sigma levels from
   0.25 to 3. Title of each subplot shows coverage at 1 sigma (ideal 0.683).
   PURPOSE: a model can look calibrated on average while being dishonest on
   one inlet type; this splits it out. FILES: cylinder_steady_production
   (only one member -- steady), cape_baseline (five members).

KNOWN FINDING (both geometries): after global recalibration the coverage
sticks near 0.95 -- the miscalibration is in the per-pixel SHAPE of sigma,
not its overall size. See pngs/model_uncertainty_vs_flow_sharpness/ for
what the shape problem looks like, and pngs/sigma_recalibration_before_after/
for what a single scale factor can and cannot fix.
