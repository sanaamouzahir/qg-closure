ERROR PERSISTENCE ACROSS THE VORTEX-SHEDDING CYCLE
==================================================
THE QUESTION THIS FOLDER ANSWERS: do the model's errors blink in and out
frame by frame, or do they stick to the flow and travel with the vortices?

EVERY PNG SHOWS: the correlation between the error field at time t and the
error field at time t + lag, as a function of lag (in simulation time
units), one line per simulation member. Vertical marks at the shedding
period T_shed (~3.0) and half of it.
FORMULA: corr(lag) = <e(t) . e(t+lag)> / (|e(t)||e(t+lag)|), averaged over
valid pixels and frame pairs, errors mean-centered.
INPUTS: best checkpoint + consecutive validation frames.
COMPARES: error decorrelation time vs the physical shedding clock.
PURPOSE: if errors stay correlated for a large fraction of the shedding
cycle, they are STRUCTURAL (the model consistently mis-handles the same
flow feature, e.g. the separating shear layer) rather than random noise --
and a rollout of the closure will drift rather than jitter. Also tells us
how independent successive training frames really are.
