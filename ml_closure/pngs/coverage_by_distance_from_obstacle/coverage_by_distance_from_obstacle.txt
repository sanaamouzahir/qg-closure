UNCERTAINTY COVERAGE AND SKILL BY DISTANCE FROM THE OBSTACLE
============================================================
THE QUESTION THIS FOLDER ANSWERS: is the model equally good (and equally
honest) near the body, in the wake, and in the free stream?

EVERY PNG HAS TWO PANELS, x = distance from the obstacle surface in units
of the obstacle size D (bins: 0-0.5, 0.5-1, 1-2, 2-4, 4-8, >8), one line
per simulation member:
  LEFT:  1-sigma coverage per distance bin (fraction of pixels whose truth
         fell within mu +/- sigma; horizontal reference at the ideal 0.683)
  RIGHT: R^2 per distance bin.
INPUTS: best checkpoint + validation frames + the signed-distance field
(the same one the model receives as an input channel).
COMPARES: skill/honesty near-body vs far-field.
PURPOSE: sub-grid forcing is concentrated near the body and in the wake;
a model that scores well globally could still be useless exactly where the
closure matters. This is the check.
