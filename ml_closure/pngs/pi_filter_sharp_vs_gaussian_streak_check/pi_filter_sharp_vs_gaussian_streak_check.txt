WHAT THIS DIAGNOSTIC IS (2026-07-13 field-plot triage, item P2)

Sanaa saw suspicious VERTICAL STREAKS in the Pi_FF field panels. This check
decides between two explanations: (a) ringing of the LES filter's sharp
spectral cutoff (a property of the filter definition — benign), or (b) a
mask/dealias bug in compute_pi_ff (which would poison the training data).

METHOD
For one developed-flow frame (t = 81, inside the 60-100 developed window),
Pi_FF was recomputed at scale s=4 in float64 twice, through the exact
production assembly (compute_pi_ff.py):
  A) production filter:  Gaussian(1.5 dx_FR)  o  sharp cutoff |kx|,|ky| < pi/dx_LES  o  average-pool
  B) test filter:        Gaussian(1.5 dx_FR)  o  Gaussian exp(-k^2 (2 dx_LES)^2 / 6) o  average-pool
Only the cutoff stage differs. If the streaks are cutoff ringing they must
vanish under (B); a masking bug would survive the filter swap.

RESULT (the verdict)
The streak cross through the obstacle vanishes COMPLETELY under the Gaussian
filter; the difference panel IS the streak pattern. Median |Pi| on the
obstacle column far from the wake: cylinder 21.7 (sharp) vs 0.09 (Gaussian),
cape 178 (sharp) vs 1.7 (Gaussian) — a collapse of about two orders of
magnitude. The recomputed sharp Pi matches the stored training product to
rel-L2 ~3e-8, so the stored data is exactly what the production filter
defines. VERDICT: the streaks are sinc/Gibbs ringing of the sharp axis-wise
cutoff acting on the huge compact obstacle-penalty (Brinkman) source. BENIGN:
not a bug, no training-data poison, the running ensemble trainings are safe.
It is a real property of the sharp-filter Pi_FF definition and belongs in the
filter-definition discussion.

Is the streak in omega_bar itself? Faintly yes — the sharp filter rings on
omega too (obstacle column 12x over background) but at only ~0.2% of the
field scale, invisible on any normal plot; under the Gaussian it is gone
(ratio 0.95, i.e. none). Pi amplifies the ringing because the filtered
Brinkman source has amplitude ~1e4 and is spatially compact.
